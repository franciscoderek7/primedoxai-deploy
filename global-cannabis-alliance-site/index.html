Our Mission

Science-Based International Reform

The Global Cannabis Alliance advocates for science-based cannabis policy reform at the international level — from UN scheduling to bilateral treaty frameworks. We work with governments, researchers, healthcare professionals, and civil society to modernize the international legal architecture governing cannabis — an architecture built in 1961 without the benefit of modern science.

Focus Areas

Policy Priorities

Four interconnected pillars driving our international advocacy work across governments, research institutions, and international bodies.

🇺🇳

UN Convention Reform

Challenging the 1961 Single Convention scheduling that classified cannabis alongside heroin — a classification unsupported by modern medical evidence or scientific consensus.

🤝

G7 Harmonization

Working toward consistent cannabis policy across G7 nations to reduce legal conflict for patients, researchers, and businesses operating in the international market.

🌍

Medical Access Equity

Ensuring medical cannabis patients in developing nations have access to the same therapeutic options available in wealthier countries. Medical access is a fundamental right.

🔬

Research Freedom

Removing barriers to international cannabis research collaboration. Science cannot advance under scheduling frameworks designed in an era of prohibition ideology.

Historic Milestone

The 2020 UN Vote

December 2020 — UN Commission on Narcotic Drugs

27
Voted YES
25
Voted NO

In December 2020, the UN Commission on Narcotic Drugs voted 27–25 to remove cannabis from Schedule IV — the most restrictive classification, the same category as heroin. Canada voted YES. This historic reclassification acknowledged decades of scientific evidence. The Global Cannabis Alliance continues to push for complete rescheduling and full treaty modernization across the international community.
