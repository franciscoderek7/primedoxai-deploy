What SoulStack Does

Six Pillars of AI Oversight

SoulStack doesn't just watch — it understands. Every observation is classified, logged, and escalated through a structured oversight framework built for multi-agent infrastructures.

🧭

Ethical Drift Detection

Monitors AI agent outputs for value misalignment, policy violations, and behavioral drift from authorized parameters. Flags agents that have strayed from their defined operating envelope.

⛓️

Authority Chain Enforcement

Ensures every AI action traces to an authorized human decision in the authority chain. Unsanctioned autonomy is blocked at the observation layer before it propagates downstream.

⚡

Real-Time Anomaly Flagging

Flags unusual patterns, unexpected outputs, and escalation attempts the moment they occur — before they reach production pipelines. Zero-latency observability across all agent communications.

🔀

Cross-Agent Contamination Watch

Monitors multi-agent pipelines for contamination propagation between agent layers. A rogue prompt in Agent A cannot silently influence Agent B when SoulStack is active.

📋

Audit Trail Generation

Complete immutable log of every agent decision, every input/output, every anomaly — timestamped and exportable. Compliance-grade records for every interaction in your agent swarm.

🚨

Human Escalation Protocol

When SoulStack detects an anomaly it cannot classify, it escalates to human oversight immediately. No unclassified anomaly ever goes unaddressed — the observer always defers upward.

FAQ

Common Questions

What exactly does "ethical drift" mean in AI systems?

▼

Ethical drift refers to the gradual or sudden deviation of an AI agent's outputs, decisions, or behaviors away from its originally authorized parameters. This can manifest as prompt injection responses, value misalignment, unauthorized scope expansion, or violation of operating constraints. SoulStack establishes a behavioral baseline at agent deployment and continuously monitors for statistically significant deviations from that baseline.

How does SoulStack integrate with existing agent pipelines?

▼

SoulStack deploys as an observation layer between your orchestration framework and your model providers. For LangChain, AutoGen, and CrewAI, we provide native SDK integrations. For custom stacks, our REST API accepts agent input/output pairs in real time. The observer is non-blocking — it does not add latency to your primary execution path. Anomalies are flagged asynchronously and escalated through your configured alert channels.

Does SoulStack block agent actions or only observe them?

▼

By default, SoulStack operates as a pure observer — it logs, classifies, and flags without blocking. On the Guardian and Sovereign tiers, you can enable enforcement mode, which allows SoulStack to intercept and hold flagged outputs for human review before they proceed. We believe the default should be observation; enforcement is opt-in and requires explicit configuration by authorized personnel.

What is "cross-agent contamination" and why does it matter?

▼

In multi-agent pipelines, one agent's output becomes another agent's input. If Agent A is compromised by a prompt injection or produces hallucinated data, and Agent B trusts that output, the contamination propagates through your entire pipeline without detection. SoulStack monitors the interfaces between agents and applies trust boundary rules, ensuring that anomalous outputs from one agent are flagged before they influence downstream agents in the swarm.

How are audit logs stored and are they tamper-proof?

▼

All audit logs are written to append-only, cryptographically hashed storage. Each log entry includes a timestamp, agent identifier, input hash, output hash, anomaly classification, and chain-of-custody signature. Logs are exportable in JSON and CSV formats. On the Sovereign tier, on-premise and air-gapped deployment options are available for organizations with strict data residency requirements.

Can SoulStack monitor agents from multiple different organizations?

▼

Yes. The Sovereign tier supports multi-tenant deployments with complete data isolation between organizational accounts. Each organization's agent data, audit logs, and anomaly reports are stored in fully isolated environments. The white-label option allows technology providers to offer SoulStack-powered monitoring to their own enterprise clients under their own brand.
