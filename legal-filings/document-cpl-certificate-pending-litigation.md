# CPL — CERTIFICATE OF PENDING LITIGATION
**Section 103 Courts of Justice Act + Rule 42 Rules of Civil Procedure**

*The CPL is registered against the LAND TITLE, not filed with the court.
It serves as notice to the world (any buyer, lender, or searcher) that a
legal claim affects this property.*

## MOTION FOR CPL — REQUIRED MATERIALS

To get the CPL issued, you must first bring a motion (or make a requisition)
supported by:

### 1. Notice of Motion for CPL (or Ex Parte Motion)

```
NOTICE OF MOTION FOR CERTIFICATE OF PENDING LITIGATION

THE PLAINTIFF WILL MAKE A MOTION for an order:

1. Directing the issuance of a Certificate of Pending Litigation pursuant to
   s. 103 of the Courts of Justice Act and Rule 42 of the Rules of Civil
   Procedure, in respect of the lands described as [LEGAL DESCRIPTION];

GROUNDS:
(a) This action is pending in this Court as above captioned;
(b) The Plaintiff's claim is in respect of an interest in land, to wit:
    [DESCRIBE — e.g., "a purchase and sale agreement / resulting trust /
    constructive trust / judgment lien arising from the within claim"];
(c) There is a reasonable possibility that the Plaintiff's claim will be
    established at trial;
(d) s. 103 Courts of Justice Act and Rule 42, Rules of Civil Procedure.

DOCUMENTARY EVIDENCE: Affidavit of Derek Francisco in support of CPL motion.
```

### 2. Affidavit in Support of CPL Motion

```
AFFIDAVIT OF DEREK FRANCISCO (in support of CPL motion)

I, Derek Francisco, swear/affirm:

1. I am the Plaintiff. I have personal knowledge of the matters herein.

2. I have commenced the within action claiming [describe claim] against the
   Defendant [Full Name] in the amount of $1,000,000.00.

3. My claim gives rise to an interest in the land described as [LEGAL DESCRIPTION]
   because: [EXPLAIN THE LEGAL BASIS — e.g.,
   "the Defendant agreed to transfer this property to me as consideration for
   [obligation]" / "a resulting trust arises from my payment of [amount] toward
   the purchase of this property" / "my judgment will be enforceable against
   this property as a judgment debtor's only substantial asset"].

4. I have a reasonable possibility of success at trial because: [BRIEF REASONS].

5. There is a real risk that absent the CPL, the Defendant will sell, mortgage,
   or otherwise encumber this property before judgment is obtained, thereby
   defeating my ability to collect on any judgment.

6. I undertake to pay any damages that the Court may award against me if it
   is ultimately found that I was not entitled to the CPL.

SWORN: _______________ BEFORE ME: _______________
```

### 3. Draft Order for CPL

```
DRAFT ORDER

IT IS ORDERED that the Local Registrar of this Court shall issue a Certificate
of Pending Litigation in respect of the following lands:

[LEGAL DESCRIPTION]

pursuant to s. 103 of the Courts of Justice Act and Rule 42 of the Rules
of Civil Procedure.

_______________________________
Justice / Registrar, S.C.J.
Date: ___________________
```

## AFTER THE CPL IS ISSUED — REGISTRATION STEPS

1. Take the issued Certificate to the **Land Registry Office** for the district
   where the land is located (City of Kawartha Lakes = **Land Titles Division —
   Victoria** at the Kawartha Lakes Land Registry, or via Teraview electronically)

2. Register the CPL on title using a **Transfer/Document Registration** in Teraview,
   OR have a licensed solicitor/title company register it on your behalf

3. Confirm registration by searching the parcel register — the CPL should appear
   as a "Certificate of Pending Litigation" instrument number

4. **Cost:** Land registration fee (based on document type) — check current
   ServiceOntario fee schedule

5. **Effect:** Once registered, any purchaser or mortgagee takes subject to your
   claim. Denby cannot sell the property with clear title while the CPL is on title.

---

## CRITICAL NOTE — UNDERTAKING AS TO DAMAGES

When a CPL is granted, the court may (and often does) require the moving party
to give an **undertaking as to damages**: if the CPL is later found to have been
wrongly obtained, you may be ordered to compensate the Defendant for any loss
caused by the CPL (e.g., a failed sale). Make sure your claim is well-founded
before registering a CPL on someone's property.

---

## DISCHARGING OR VACATING A CPL

The Defendant can bring a motion to discharge the CPL under Rule 42.02.
Be prepared to defend your claim to the property interest at that hearing.
