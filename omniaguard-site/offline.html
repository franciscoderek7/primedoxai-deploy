Emergency Charter Reference (Cached)
Charter s.8

Search & Seizure

Protection against unreasonable search and seizure. A search without prior judicial authorization is presumptively unreasonable. The Crown bears the burden to rebut.

Key case: R v Hunter [1984] — R v Spencer [2014]
Charter s.10(b)

Right to Counsel

On arrest or detention, state clearly: "I wish to speak with a lawyer." Police must immediately cease questioning. Waiver of this right must be knowing and voluntary.

Key case: R v Manninen [1987] — R v Bartle [1994]
Charter s.24(2)

Exclusion of Evidence

Grant 2009 three-part test: (1) seriousness of Charter-infringing conduct, (2) impact on accused's Charter-protected interests, (3) society's interest in adjudication on merits.

Key case: R v Grant [2009] SCC 32
Charter s.7

Life, Liberty & Security

Any state action threatening life, liberty, or security of the person must comply with principles of fundamental justice. Medical necessity defense engages s.7 directly.

Key case: Canada v Bedford [2013] — Carter [2015]
Charter s.11(b)

Trial in Reasonable Time

R v Jordan 2016 presumptive ceiling: 18 months (Provincial Court), 30 months (Superior Court). Track every adjournment — Crown-caused delays count fully toward ceiling.

Key case: R v Jordan [2016] SCC 27
Disclosure

Stinchcombe Right

Crown must disclose all relevant material — inculpatory and exculpatory. Demand in writing at the first appearance. Gaps in disclosure trigger a stay application under s.24(1).

Key case: R v Stinchcombe [1991] 3 SCR 326
