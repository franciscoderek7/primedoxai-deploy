Charter Rights Quick Reference
Charter s.7

Life, Liberty & Security

Life, liberty and security of the person. Medical necessity defense foundation. Engages when state action threatens these fundamental interests.

• R v Morgentaler [1988] — security of person
• Rodriguez v BC [1993] — life & bodily integrity
• Canada v Bedford [2013] — s.7 + Oakes test
• Carter v Canada [2015] — medical autonomy
Charter s.8

Unreasonable Search & Seizure

Protection against unreasonable search and seizure. Evidence exclusion under Grant 2009. Requires reasonable expectation of privacy.

• R v Hunter [1984] — reasonable expectation
• R v Tessling [2004] — privacy framework
• R v Spencer [2014] — digital privacy
• R v Fearon [2014] — cell phone search
Charter s.10(b)

Right to Counsel

Right to retain and instruct counsel without delay. Invoke immediately and clearly. State must cease questioning until counsel consulted.

• R v Manninen [1987] — cease questioning
• R v Bartle [1994] — duty to inform
• R v Sinclair [2010] — scope at trial
• R v McCrimmon [2010] — waiver standard
Charter s.12

Cruel & Unusual Treatment

Protection against cruel and unusual treatment or punishment. Disproportionate penalties. Grossly disproportionate sentences challenged here.

• R v Smith [1987] — gross disproportionality test
• R v Lyons [1987] — dangerous offender provisions
• R v Lloyd [2016] — mandatory minimums struck
Charter s.15

Equal Protection of Law

Equal protection and benefit of the law. Systemic discrimination defense. Analogous grounds analysis for novel protected groups.

• Andrews v Law Society [1989] — equality framework
• Egan v Canada [1995] — analogous grounds
• Law v Canada [1999] — dignity test
• R v Kapp [2008] — revised test
Charter s.24(2)

Exclusion of Evidence

Exclusion of evidence obtained in Charter violations. Grant 3-part test: seriousness of breach, impact on accused, society's interest.

• R v Grant [2009] — definitive 3-part test
• R v Harrison [2009] — balancing approach
• R v Morelli [2010] — digital evidence
• R v Paterson [2017] — good faith factor
BENO-X Framework

Bureaucratic Error Neutralization

Challenge every procedural defect first. Disclosure errors, limitation periods, jurisdictional defects. Administrative errors often nullify proceedings before the merits.

• Challenge validity of charging document
• Demand complete Crown disclosure
• Identify limitation period defects
• Challenge jurisdiction over person or subject matter
• Constitutional challenge under s.52
12-Appearance Sovereignty Framework

Strategic delay and procedural challenge at each court appearance. Each appearance is an opportunity — not an obligation to proceed.
