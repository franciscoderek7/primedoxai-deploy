Regulatory History

MMAR / ACMPR History

Medical Cannabis in Canada — A Judicial Mandate

Medical cannabis access in Canada was not a gift from Parliament — it was established through judicial mandate. The R. v. Parker decision (2000) found that denying access to medical cannabis to a patient with a medical need violated the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms. The government was compelled to create a legal framework.

The resulting regulatory history has evolved significantly over two decades:

2000
R. v. Parker — Ontario Court of Appeal strikes down simple possession for medical necessity.
2001
Marihuana Medical Access Regulations (MMAR) — first federal framework established.
2013
Marihuana for Medical Purposes Regulations (MMPR) — shifts from personal production to licensed commercial production.
2016
Access to Cannabis for Medical Purposes Regulations (ACMPR) — restores personal production rights following Allard v. Canada court decision.
2018
Cannabis Act — adult use legalization. Medical framework preserved and integrated.
