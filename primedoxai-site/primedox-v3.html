BENO-X Framework — Constitutional Defense AI

Three-step constitutional challenge framework developed through decades of Canadian case law. Powered by LexMind.

1
Does an Exemption Exist?
R v Parker, 2000 — Ontario Court of Appeal
▼

The Parker decision established that a blanket prohibition on cannabis without a medical exemption violates s.7 of the Charter — the right to life, liberty and security of the person. LexMind analyzes whether a viable exemption pathway exists under current federal regulations, and whether that exemption covers your specific circumstances. The BENO-X module cross-references your file against Parker criteria in under 2 seconds.

2
Is the Exemption Functional?
R v Allard, 2016 FC 236 — Federal Court of Canada
▼

R v Allard confirmed that a technically existing exemption which is practically inaccessible or unreasonably burdensome is constitutionally defective. If Health Canada's ACMPR or comparable framework creates financial, geographic, or procedural barriers that render the exemption functionally unavailable, the prohibition remains vulnerable to Charter challenge. LexMind maps exemption access barriers against Allard criteria and generates a viability score for your jurisdiction.

3
Is the Prohibition Constitutionally Sound?
R v Smith, 2015 SCC 34 — Supreme Court of Canada
▼

Smith (2015) struck down the restriction limiting medical cannabis to dried form only, finding it violated s.7 of the Charter in a manner not justified under s.1. The Supreme Court affirmed that where a prohibition is overly broad and captures conduct posing no real risk of harm, it cannot be saved by pressing and substantial government objectives. The BENO-X Step 3 engine runs a Smith analysis on the specific charge, seeking overbreadth, gross disproportionality, and arbitrariness under s.7.
