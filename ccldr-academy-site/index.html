Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ

Will this certification get me legal jobs or legal credentials?

No. CCLDR Academy certifications are educational credentials only. They do not constitute legal qualifications, do not authorize educational guidance, and are not recognized by any Law Society. CCLDR Academy is not for lawyers — lawyers have law school.

Is this recognized by Law Societies or government bodies?

No. CCLDR Academy is an independent educational institution. Our certifications are not recognized by Law Societies of Canada, provincial bar associations, or any regulatory body. This is an educational program, not a professional licensing body.

What can I actually do with a CCLDR certification?

You can educate, advocate, and demonstrate serious constitutional literacy in your community. Many students use CCLDR certification to teach workshops, support advocacy organizations, assist community members in understanding their rights, and demonstrate commitment to cannabis law education.

Is this curriculum updated as laws change?

Yes. The CCLDR curriculum is reviewed and updated as Canadian cannabis legislation and relevant Charter jurisprudence evolves. Certified graduates are notified of material curriculum changes.

Who creates and grades the examinations?

Examinations are developed and graded by Derek Francisco (Doc Weedlaw), the curriculum author. Oral reviews for Sovereign Graduate certification are conducted personally.
