The Clerk's Office

Filing procedures, fee schedule, step-by-step guide for self-represented litigants in Ontario courts. No lawyer required to understand this.

📝How to File a Claim in Ontario
1
Draft Your Statement of Claim (Form 14A)
Use the template in the Records Room (Elite tier) or download Form 14A from ontario.ca/courts. Include: parties, facts, legal basis, and what you're asking for.
💡 CCLDR Records Room has pre-filled templates for cannabis-related claims
2
Go to the Courthouse — File Your Claim
Bring 3 copies of your Statement of Claim to the court registrar. File at the courthouse in the county where the defendant lives or where the incident occurred.
📍 Kawartha Lakes: City of Kawartha Lakes Courthouse, Lindsay, Ontario
3
Pay the Filing Fee
Fees are calculated based on your claim amount (see fee calculator). Pay by cash, cheque (payable to Minister of Finance), or debit/credit at some locations.
4
Serve the Defendant
The court will stamp your claim with a file number. You must serve the defendant personally (or by other approved methods) within 6 months of issuing the claim.
⚠️ Service rules are strict — improper service can result in dismissal
5
Wait for the Defence (20 Days)
Defendant has 20 days to file a Statement of Defence (if served in Ontario). If no defence filed, you can request Default Judgment.
6
Exchange Documents (Discovery)
Both parties must exchange relevant documents within 10 days of close of pleadings. Consider Notice of Examination (Form 31A) to question the defendant under oath.
7
Attend Mandatory Mediation
In Toronto, Ottawa, and Windsor — mandatory for most civil claims. Check if your county requires it. Good opportunity to settle without trial.
8
Pre-Trial Conference → Trial
A judge meets both parties to narrow issues. If no settlement, trial is scheduled. Prepare your witnesses, exhibits, and opening/closing arguments.
💡 CCLDR Boardroom (Professional tier) has trial preparation worksheets

✅Pre-Filing Checklist
  • Statement of Claim drafted (Form 14A)
  • Claim amount calculated and confirmed
  • Defendant's full legal name and address confirmed
  • 3 copies of Statement of Claim printed
  • Filing fee ready (cash or cheque to Minister of Finance)
  • Courthouse location and hours confirmed
  • Limitation period checked (within 2 years?)
  • Evidence organized and labelled (Exhibit A, B, C...)
  • Witnesses identified and available
  • Process server arranged (or plan for personal service)
  • Lawyer consulted (strongly recommended)
