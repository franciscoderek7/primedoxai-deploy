Hearing Preparation Checklist
Tap each item to mark complete. Progress is saved automatically to this browser.
Pre-Hearing — 7 Days Before
  • Serve all documents on opposing party — confirm service and obtain proof
  • Confirm court date, time, courtroom number, and presiding judge
  • Organize and tab all exhibits in binder — label each with exhibit number
  • File any outstanding materials with the court registry
  • Review opponent's materials — identify weaknesses and inconsistencies
Pre-Hearing — 48 Hours Before
  • Confirm witness attendance — send reminder with courthouse address and time
  • Review all opposing materials filed — prepare specific responses
  • Prepare and rehearse opening statement (target: 3–5 minutes)
  • Print 3 copies of all documents (one for you, one for judge, one for opposing party)
  • Research judge's prior decisions on the issues raised — note tendencies
Day of Hearing
  • Arrive at courthouse 30 minutes early — locate your courtroom
  • Bring 3 copies of every document — organized in labelled binders
  • Dress professionally — business attire signals respect for the court
  • Turn phone to silent — switch off notifications before entering courtroom
  • Sign in at the clerk's desk and confirm your matter is on the list
In the Courtroom
  • Stand when the judge enters and when addressed — sit only when instructed
  • Address the judge as "Your Honour" at all times — never by name
  • Speak slowly and clearly — pause between points to allow the judge to write
  • Never interrupt opposing counsel or the judge — wait your turn
  • Take notes of everything said — ask to repeat if you miss anything
