The BENO-X Defense Framework

Bad Exemption = No Offense — Educational Legal Framework

"Bad Exemption = No Offense" — BENO-X Framework
Education Only
Parker 2000
R v Parker, 2000 CanLII 5762 (ON CA)

A landmark Ontario Court of Appeal decision that found the federal marijuana prohibition unconstitutional as it applied to individuals with serious medical conditions who had no viable legal access to cannabis. The court held that the lack of a lawful alternative created an unjustifiable burden under s.7 of the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms. This case forms the historical cornerstone of the BENO-X educational framework.

Education Only
Allard 2016
Allard v Canada, 2016 FC 236

The Federal Court of Canada struck down the Marihuana for Medical Purposes Regulations (MMPR), ruling that forcing patients to obtain cannabis exclusively from licensed commercial producers — and prohibiting home production — infringed their right to liberty and security of person under s.7 of the Charter. The decision reinforced the principle that a regulatory exemption that is functionally inaccessible is constitutionally inadequate.

Education Only
Smith 2015 SCC 34
R v Smith, 2015 SCC 34, [2015] 2 SCR 602

The Supreme Court of Canada unanimously held that a restriction limiting medical cannabis authorizations to dried plant material — and thereby prohibiting derivative forms such as oils and edibles — was constitutionally invalid. The court found this limit on form of consumption violated s.7 of the Charter, as it forced patients to choose between adequate treatment and compliance with the law. Smith 2015 SCC 34 is a critical precedent studied within the BENO-X educational curriculum.
