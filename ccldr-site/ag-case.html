Constitutional Foundation

Charter Grounds

The claim is grounded in four sections of the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms. Each section was violated by the conduct of the Attorney General of Canada and Health Canada in relation to Derek Francisco's medical cannabis rights and personal liberty.

§7
Life · Liberty · Security
Right to Life, Liberty, and Security of the Person
Everyone has the right to life, liberty and security of the person and the right not to be deprived thereof except in accordance with the principles of fundamental justice.
Applied here: Regulatory interference with Derek Francisco's access to medical cannabis directly jeopardized his health and security of person, depriving him of a fundamental liberty without just process.
§9
Arbitrary Detention
Right Not to Be Arbitrarily Detained or Imprisoned
Everyone has the right not to be arbitrarily detained or imprisoned.
Applied here: Enforcement actions and regulatory interference constituting arbitrary deprivation of liberty and freedom of movement without legal justification.
§12
Cruel & Unusual Treatment
Right Not to Be Subjected to Cruel and Unusual Punishment
Everyone has the right not to be subjected to any cruel and unusual treatment or punishment.
Applied here: Systematic denial of medical cannabis access to a person with established medical need constitutes cruel and unusual treatment under s.12 of the Charter.
§15
Equality Rights
Right to Equal Treatment Under the Law
Every individual is equal before and under the law and has the right to equal protection and equal benefit of the law without discrimination.
Applied here: Discriminatory enforcement against cannabis users while treating equivalent conduct by others differently — violation of equality rights and the right to equal benefit of the law.
