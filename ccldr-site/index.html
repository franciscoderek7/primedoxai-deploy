Charter of Rights and Freedoms

The Sections That Protect You

Every cannabis charge triggers multiple Charter rights. Knowing these is the foundation of every defense strategy.

s.7

Life, Liberty & Security

The backbone of cannabis jurisprudence. R v Parker, Smith, Allard — all won on s.7. Medical access is a constitutional right.

s.8

Unreasonable Search

Was your search lawful? Reasonable and probable grounds? A bad search = evidence excluded. Most cannabis charges turn on this section.

s.9

Arbitrary Detention

Arbitrary stops to search for cannabis may violate s.9. Combined with s.8 violations, this can end a prosecution entirely.

s.10(b)

Right to Counsel

Upon arrest you must be told your right to a lawyer immediately. Any violation taints everything that follows. Exercise this right immediately.

s.11(d)

Presumption of Innocence

The Crown proves guilt beyond reasonable doubt. You don't prove innocence. Understand this distinction before you say a single word.

s.24

Charter Remedies

s.24(1) = just remedy. s.24(2) = exclude illegally obtained evidence. A well-argued s.24 motion can stay proceedings entirely.

BENO-X

Bad Exemption = No Offense

If the government's exemption regime (MMAR → MMPR → ACMPR) fails to provide constitutionally adequate access to cannabis, then possession outside that defective regime cannot be prosecuted as a criminal offense. Five landmark cases prove it.

12-Appearance Methodology

Every Stage of Your Case — Mapped

Most people don't know what's happening in court. The 12-Appearance framework gives you an educational map of every stage from arrest to resolution — and what rights apply at each step.

STAGE 01
First Appearance / Bail

Bail conditions set. s.10b is critical here. Do not speak without a lawyer.

STAGE 02
Remand / Disclosure Order

Crown ordered to disclose full investigation. The Jordan clock may start here.

STAGE 03
Disclosure Review

Defence reviews disclosure for gaps, Charter violations, investigative deficiencies.

STAGE 04
Crown Pre-Trial

Negotiation with Crown. Withdrawal, diversion, or reduced resolution discussed.

STAGE 05
Judicial Pre-Trial

Judge-facilitated discussion of issues, guilty plea options, trial efficiency.

STAGE 06
Election / Set Date

Election: judge alone, judge+jury, or preliminary inquiry request.

STAGE 07
Charter Applications

s.8, s.9, s.10b, s.11b motions served. The most critical defense stage.

STAGE 08–12
Hearings → Trial → Appeal

Charter hearings, trial, verdict, sentencing, and full appeal rights analysis.

What is the BENO-X Framework?
+
BENO-X stands for "Bad Exemption = No Offense." It's an educational constitutional framework built on five landmark Canadian cases: Parker 2000, Hitzig 2003, Smith 2015, Allard 2016, and ongoing 2023 FC litigation. The argument: if the government's exemption regime (MMAR/MMPR/ACMPR) has been found constitutionally defective, possession outside that defective regime may not constitute a criminal offense. BENO-X synthesizes 20+ years of jurisprudence into a structured educational model.
What is the 12-Appearance Methodology?
+
The 12-Appearance Methodology is an educational framework mapping approximately 12 distinct court stages in a typical cannabis criminal matter — from first appearance/bail hearing through trial and appeal. Each stage includes which Charter rights apply, what typically happens procedurally, and what strategic considerations exist. Designed to demystify the court process for non-lawyers.
