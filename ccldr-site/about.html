Year It Started

Derek Francisco — known to thousands of Canadians as Doc WeedLaw — has spent over two decades navigating the intersection of cannabis law and constitutional rights. A self-represented litigant since 2006, Derek didn't start as an educator. He started as a defendant.

Facing his first cannabis charge in 2006, Derek made a decision that would define the next twenty years: he would learn the system, challenge it constitutionally, and share everything he discovered. That decision created CCLDR.NET and the BENO-X Framework — the most comprehensive Canadian cannabis constitutional defense resource ever assembled.

Over the years, Derek has educated 200+ Canadians on their Charter rights, built a library of 3,000+ resources, and filed landmark constitutional challenges including Francisco v. Attorney General — a direct challenge to the Cannabis Act itself. He has supported veterans like Bill Denby, fighting PTSD-related cannabis charges with the same constitutional rigor he applies to every case.

"Not a lawyer. Better." That's not arrogance — it's a statement of purpose. Lawyers are constrained by professional rules, liability, and billable hours. Derek is constrained by nothing but the Constitution. And that makes him dangerous in the best possible way.
