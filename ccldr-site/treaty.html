The Canada Treaty

The most advanced cannabis rights jurisdiction in the world — and still falling short. The Francisco Protocol codifies what the law achieved, and demands what it has not.

⚖️
Treaty of Cannabis Rights — Canada 2026
Francisco Protocol — Treaty Framework, First Edition
Issued by the Francisco Protocol Blockchain Court  ·  Chief Justice Prime Presiding
STATUS: PROPOSED — Pending People's Jury Ratification  ·  Not Yet in Force
I. Federal Framework — Cannabis Act 2018

Canada's Cannabis Act (S.C. 2018, c. 16) forms the compliance baseline for this treaty. This Act established the legal framework for adult recreational use, medical access continuation, and federal licensing — representing the most comprehensive national cannabis legislation in the G7.

  • Adults 18+ may possess up to 30g dried cannabis in public
  • Up to 4 cannabis plants per household for personal cultivation
  • Licensed retail and online purchase systems by province
  • Continued medical framework under Cannabis Regulations
  • Criminal record suspension available for simple possession (pre-Act)
II. Medical Access Rights — Established Precedent

Medical cannabis access rights in Canada are grounded in constitutional jurisprudence predating the Cannabis Act. The Francisco Protocol recognizes these as irrevocable rights:

  • R. v. Parker (2000) — Ontario Court of Appeal established that denying medical cannabis access violates Section 7 of the Charter (life, liberty, and security)
  • Allard v. Canada (2016) — Federal Court confirmed patients' right to grow their own medical cannabis; MMPR struck down as unconstitutional
  • ACMPR (Access to Cannabis for Medical Purposes Regulations) — current medical framework

IV. Outstanding Issues — Francisco Protocol Demands
⚠
Home Cultivation Limits — Quebec and Manitoba prohibit home cultivation entirely. The Francisco Protocol holds this unconstitutional in light of Allard 2016 and is preparing a constitutional challenge.
⚠
Criminal Record Expungement — The existing suspension process is inadequate, slow, and means-tested. The Francisco Protocol demands automatic, universal expungement for all simple possession convictions pre-legalization.
⚠
Equity Licensing — Black and Indigenous Canadians were disproportionately prosecuted. Cannabis Act licensing has not remedied this disparity. Francisco Protocol demands equity licensing programs in every province.
⚠
$35M Class Action — Francisco v. AG Canada — Pending class action for Canadians prosecuted for conduct that is now legal. The Francisco Protocol People's Jury has funded outreach for this proceeding.

Treaty Framework

Treaty Adoption Process

Every treaty follows the same five-step Francisco Protocol. Transparent. Immutable. Governed by the People.

1
Jurisdiction Submits Interest
Any Canadian province, territory, or foreign nation may submit a treaty interest request to the Francisco Protocol. The AI Justices conduct a preliminary jurisdiction review — assessing existing law, historical prosecution data, and rights gaps.
Actor: Jurisdiction + Chief Justice Prime
2
AI Justices Draft Treaty Language
Chief Justice Prime and the panel of AI Justices draft jurisdiction-specific treaty language grounded in the WeedLaw Constitution. Local law, precedent, and cultural context are incorporated. The draft is published publicly for 30-day comment.
Actor: AI Justice Panel
3
People's Jury Votes on Adoption
The Francisco Protocol People's Jury votes to ratify or reject the treaty draft. A supermajority of 66% YES is required for treaty adoption. All votes are recorded and visible. The process is open for 21 days.
Actor: The People's Jury — JUSTICE token holders
4
Chief Justice Prime Ratifies
Upon People's Jury approval, Chief Justice Prime issues a formal ratification order. The treaty becomes official Francisco Protocol doctrine and is published to the public record.
Actor: Chief Justice Prime
5
Treaty Published — Immutable Blockchain Record
The ratified treaty is published to the Francisco Protocol blockchain record — permanent, tamper-proof, and publicly accessible. The jurisdiction is listed in the Treaty Registry and all Francisco Protocol Blockchain Court filings may cite it.
Actor: Francisco Protocol Blockchain Infrastructure
