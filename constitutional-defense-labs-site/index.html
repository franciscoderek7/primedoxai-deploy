R v Parker — 2000 CanLII 5762 (ON CA)

R v Parker

2000 — Ontario Court of Appeal

A foundational ruling on medical necessity and the constitutionality of cannabis prohibition. The court struck down the criminal prohibition as unconstitutional in the absence of a medical exemption, establishing that s.7 of the Charter protects access to necessary medical treatment.

  • Section 7 right to life and security of the person engaged by criminal prohibition
  • Medical necessity as a constitutional consideration under the Charter
  • Prohibition struck as unconstitutional without medical access framework
  • Established foundation for subsequent medical cannabis regulation in Canada
Charter: s.7 — Life, Liberty & Security of the Person
R v Smith — 2015 SCC 34

R v Smith

2015 — Supreme Court of Canada

The Supreme Court unanimously struck down Health Canada's restriction limiting medical cannabis to dried form only. The Court held the prohibition on non-dried forms violated s.7 by forcing patients to choose between their liberty and their health.

  • Unconstitutional to restrict medical cannabis to a single consumption method
  • Liberty and security cannot be arbitrarily constrained by regulatory design
  • Health Canada's regime found grossly disproportionate to its objective
  • Expanded the medical access framework to all cannabis forms
Charter: s.7 — Liberty & Security of the Person
R v Grant — 2009 SCC 32

R v Grant

2009 — Supreme Court of Canada

The Supreme Court reformulated the test for excluding evidence obtained through Charter breaches under s.24(2). The Grant test replaced the Collins framework and established a three-step balancing analysis that remains the standard today.

  • Established the modern s.24(2) three-part evidence exclusion test
  • Seriousness of state Charter-infringing conduct is primary consideration
  • Impact on the accused's Charter-protected interests weighs heavily
  • Society's interest in adjudication on merits is balancing factor
Charter: s.9, s.10(b), s.24(2) — Evidence Exclusion
