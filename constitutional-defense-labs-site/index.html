About CDL

The Research Arm

Constitutional Defense Labs is the research arm of the Francisco Holdings legal education portfolio. We study Charter jurisprudence, develop analytical frameworks, and publish educational resources designed to advance understanding of constitutional rights in Canada.

Our work focuses on s.7 of the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms, regulatory exemption analysis, and the development of methodologies — including the BENO-X framework — that support constitutional education for Canadians. We are not a law firm and do not provide legal representation or legal advice.

CDL operates within the Francisco Holdings innovation portfolio based in Lindsay, Ontario, with research activities that may qualify for Scientific Research and Experimental Development (SR&ED) tax incentives under the Income Tax Act.

Active Research

Current Projects

Four active research streams advancing Charter education and analytical methodology.

Active

Charter s.7 Analysis Database

Cataloguing fundamental justice jurisprudence from 1982 to present. A systematic index of s.7 cases with analytical summaries, principled frameworks, and cross-references to regulatory law.

Active

BENO-X Framework v3.0

Third-generation exemption analysis methodology. Building on Parker, Smith, and Allard, this iteration incorporates procedural jurisprudence and expanded regulatory defect typologies.

Active

12-Appearance Timeline Study

Procedural defense optimization research examining the strategic and constitutional implications of case management timelines in the Ontario Court of Justice. Educational analysis only.

Research

AI-Assisted Charter Research

Technology tools for constitutional education — exploring how AI-powered research assistants can improve access to Charter jurisprudence for self-represented individuals and researchers.
